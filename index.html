THE DAILY TROJAN

Empty Campus

With more than 48,000 students spanning two campuses in the heart of Los Angeles, USC boasts an energized, hardworking student body, which moves in a constant frenzy from class to work every day.

This usual campus rhythm has since transformed to a humdrum due to the coronavirus pandemic. Save for the dozens of essential workers and more than 1,000 students still on campus, typical hotspots, leisure areas and study spaces are left barren. From the bustling Trousdale Parkway to the tucked-away Scene Dock Theatre, nearly all activity across the University has been erased. With the status of in-person classes for the fall semester uncertain, here is a glimpse into what our campus has looked like while we’ve been away.

Hecuba

Little human presence is found in the Village, even on bright, sunny days.

Trousdale Parkway

One of the busiest parts of campus on a normal day, Tommy Trojan has gotten almost no visitors recently.

McCarthy Quad

The pathway students take to get to class and the location of numerous concerts and festivals at USC, McCarthy Quad now serves neither purpose.

Childs Way

The narrow Childs Way typically causes a frustrating traffic jam for students who are walking, biking and boarding to class. Now, scurrying feet and wheels are nowhere in sight.

Village Square

The picnic tables at USC Village, normally a popular lunch spot and gathering place for students, now sit alone, vacant.

USC Village

The West Jefferson intersection acted as a gateway for students grabbing a bite to eat, shopping or returning to their apartments in the Village.

Starbucks at USC Village

Usually bustling with caffeine-craving students, Starbucks in USC Village is desolate, the hum of the machines gone quiet.

West 34th Street

En route to USC Village, West 34th Street was a hub for USC buses headed toward the Health Sciences Campus, USC City Center and Union Station.

VKC

The only movement outside these once busy classrooms is the waving of the flags.

Fertitta Hall

The sun shines on the empty courtyard in front of Fertitta Hall, the doors of which remain untouched.

USC Bookstore

The steps of USC Bookstore no longer host quick afternoon lunches or meetups with friends.

Annenberg

A hotspot for students heading to class, the building receives only a few passerbys.

Watt Way

A street inside campus that is now absent of cars and students.

Engineering Quad

Previously a quiet study spot, the e-quad is now completely desolate.

Parkside

Students swarmed this dining hall for its food and soft-serve ice cream. Now, only the Village dining hall is open.

Dedeaux Field

Safer-at-home orders don’t include home plate, as the best seats in the house at Dedeaux Field lie empty.

Hahn Plaza

Traveler overlooks a deserted Student Union and a Hahn Plaza Fountain that has since stopped flowing.

Ronald Tutor Campus Center

The umbrellas outside the Ronald Tutor Campus Center remain closed, no longer providing shade to students basking under the L.A. sun.

Taper Hall

The epicenter of USC’s English and literature classes, the book is closed on Taper Hall this spring.
